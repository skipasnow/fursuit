Protecting the Investment

A custom costume is a massive financial and artistic investment. Without proper, immediate care after every single use, the faux fur will mat permanently, backing fabrics will degrade, and bacteria will ruin the internal padding. Establishing a strict maintenance routine and assembling a dedicated travel and care kit is non-negotiable for longevity.

Logistics & Transport

Whether taking a domestic hop or a long-haul international flight across the Pacific, how you pack dictates the survival of the suit. For airline travel, the Pelican 0340 Cube Case is the industry-standard recommendation. It is engineered to provide the maximum allowable volume for a checked bag while remaining within standard size constraints.

PELICAN 0340 SPECIFICATIONS:
• Exterior: 20.5 x 20.5 x 19.25 in (52.1 x 52.1 x 48.9 cm)
• Interior: 18.00 x 18.00 x 18.00 in (45.7 x 45.7 x 45.7 cm)
SIZE LIMIT WARNING: If a fursuit head exceeds 18 inches in any dimension, it is not advisable for air transportation. Forcing a rigid head into a smaller container risks permanent deformation or cracking. Foam and TPU bases can be marginally compressed for several hours without deformation, but Resin and PLA are brittle and will break or deform from extended pressure.

Vacuum Compression Bags

  • Body suits and padding take up massive volume. Use heavy-duty vacuum seal bags (the kind requiring a hand pump or vacuum) to compress the body down to a fraction of its size.
  • Warning: Do not leave fur vacuum-sealed for longer than the duration of the flight, as it can permanently kink the fur fibers. Unpack and brush immediately upon arrival.

The Base Care Kit

Purchase these items before your suit arrives.

The Brushes

  • Slicker Brush: A wire-bristle pet brush. Used to detangle matted fur. Always brush gently to avoid pulling fibers out of the backing.
  • Rubber Brush: Great for gathering loose shedding fibers without damaging the suit.

Microfiber & Lens Care

  • For large plastic eyes, carry premium microfiber cloths and anti-fog lens cleaner. Never use paper towels or harsh chemicals on the eyes, as they will scratch and cloud the plastic.

Portable Ventilation

  • A high-velocity, small-form-factor floor fan is critical. Costume heads contain foam and dense fabrics that trap moisture. Resting the head neck-down over a fan immediately after wear prevents mold.

The DIY Disinfectant (Precision Formula)

Commercial sprays are often diluted. For maximum efficacy and safety, use a pure Isopropyl base. This formula eliminates the need for distilled water, ensuring a faster evaporation rate and better antimicrobial action.

> MASTER MIXTURE RATIO: • Base: 70% Isopropyl Alcohol (Use pure; do not dilute further with water).
• Scent: 1ml of IFRA-compliant (skin-safe) fragrance oil per 1 fl oz of alcohol.

> WHY 70%? At this concentration, the alcohol permeates the cell wall of bacteria more effectively than 99% concentrations, which often evaporate too quickly to fully disinfect.
> APPLICATION PROTOCOL You must only ever spray the inside of the fursuit. The objective is strictly to sanitize the interior lining and padding that have direct exposure to human sweat and waste materials. Do not spray or interact with the exterior faux fur in any way if possible, as the alcohol mixture can degrade or damage the synthetic fibers over time. Let the interior sit in front of a high-powered fan until completely bone dry before storing.
> SAFETY COMPLIANCE Always ensure your fragrance oils are IFRA compliant. Since the suit lining sits directly against your skin during high-exertion activity, non-compliant oils can cause chemical burns or severe contact dermatitis.

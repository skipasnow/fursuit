Suit Maintenance & Logistics

Protecting the Investment

A custom costume is a massive financial and artistic investment. Without proper, immediate care after every single use, the faux fur will mat permanently, backing fabrics will degrade, and bacteria will ruin the internal padding. Establishing a strict maintenance routine and assembling a dedicated travel and care kit is non-negotiable for longevity.

Logistics & Transport

Whether taking a domestic hop or a long-haul international flight across the Pacific, how you pack dictates the survival of the suit.

Hard-Shell Luggage

  • The Head: Never check a costume head in a soft duffel. It requires a hard-shell suitcase (polycarbonate) to prevent crushing.
  • Dome Eyes: Anime-style suits often feature glossy plastic domes. Always wrap the head in a soft fleece blanket or custom hood to prevent the domes from scratching against the suitcase lining.

Vacuum Compression Bags

  • Body suits and padding take up massive volume. Use heavy-duty vacuum seal bags (the kind requiring a hand pump or vacuum) to compress the body down to a fraction of its size.
  • Warning: Do not leave fur vacuum-sealed for longer than the duration of the flight, as it can permanently kink the fur fibers. Unpack and brush immediately upon arrival.

The Base Care Kit

Purchase these items before your suit arrives.

The Brushes

  • Slicker Brush: A wire-bristle pet brush. Used to detangle matted fur. Always brush gently to avoid pulling fibers out of the backing.
  • Rubber Brush: Great for gathering loose shedding fibers without damaging the suit.

Microfiber & Lens Care

  • For large plastic eyes, carry premium microfiber cloths and anti-fog lens cleaner. Never use paper towels or harsh chemicals on the eyes, as they will scratch and cloud the plastic.

Portable Ventilation

  • A high-velocity, small-form-factor floor fan is critical. Costume heads contain foam and dense fabrics that trap moisture. Resting the head neck-down over a fan immediately after wear prevents mold.

The DIY Disinfectant (Money Saver)

Commercial "fursuit sprays" are incredibly expensive for the volume you receive. You can create a highly effective, fabric-safe antibacterial spray at home for pennies on the dollar. The goal is to kill bacteria (which causes odor) and evaporate quickly.

> OPTIMAL MIXTURE RATIO: • 50% Isopropyl Alcohol (At least 70% concentration)
• 50% Distilled Water (Must be distilled to prevent mineral buildup)
• 10-15 Drops of Essential Oil (Avoid heavily tinted oils like sweet orange, which can stain white fur. Lavender, tea tree, or clear eucalyptus are best).

> INSTRUCTIONS: Mix in a continuous-mist spray bottle. Shake well before each use.
> APPLICATION PROTOCOL Spray the inside of the suit (the lining and foam) thoroughly immediately after doffing. Never spray the exterior fur heavily with alcohol, as it can dry out acrylic fibers over time. Let it sit in front of a high-powered fan until completely bone dry before storing.
